Multivariate Map - Social Vulnerability, Nitrate Exposure and Colorectal Cancer Risk

Environmental pollution affects everyone in the place, but not everyone is affected equally. Social Vulnerability analysis aims to identify the population in places that are more susceptible to environmental hazard. This could help policymakers better prioritize resources for the vulnerable population.

This map helps you explore the multivariate relationships among relative risk of colorectal cancer, nitrate concentration, clinical care and different socio-demographic characteristics in Iowa state by census tract level.

  • The choropleth map is symbolized by the variable from the categories of cancer risk, nitrate exposure and demographics with quantile classification. You can change the variable by selecting it from the dropdown menu on the right side. If you want to know the details of a tract, you can hover your mouse on the tracts in the map. The hovered tract will be highlighted with black border and the correspoingding line on the parallel coordinates will be emphasized by setting other variables grey.
  • The parallel coordinates below the map visualize the value of each variable at each census tract. You can brush the variable axes to select ideal value ranges and explore the distribution of these values. If you want to clear the selection, you can click on the axis outside of the selected range.
  • You can find the explanation of each variable by hovering over the "Variable Information" button on the right side.

Questions:

  1. Where are people experiencing high nitrate pollution (>5mg/L) exposure?
  2. How does the clinical care rankings vary across these regions? Additionally, which areas have a relatively low level of clinical care?
  3. What are the population characteristics of those areas?
  4. Which locations exhibit both elevated nitrate concentrations and a high risk of colorectal cancer? Does the population characteristics change in these areas?
  5. Do you find any outliers?
