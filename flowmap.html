Inter-Provincial Migration and Economic Disparities in China, 2010

Flow map project file

This map illustrates the inter-provincial migration and economic disparities in China in 2010. The choropleth map is symbolized with quantile classification 1 of Gross Domestic Production (GDP) per capita in each province. The color changes from light to dark red to symbolize the low to high values. It reveals the economic status of each administrative region and economic disparities across the country. The proportional node symbols represent the gross inflow of population in each province with quantile classification. It indicates the degree of attraction of each province to migrants from other provinces. The graduated flow symbols are symbolized with quantile classification of modularity of the migration flow between provinces. Modularity represents normalized flow by reducing the effect of population size. The graduate increase of thickness and blackness presents higher modularity, emphasizing the increment in value. The choropleth map, node symbols and flow symbols are classified with quantile method for comparison among the value distribution to find relationships. 100 top flows are shown to limit the visual clutter caused by overlapping flow lines.

The flow pattern indicates three insights. Firstly, the top 100 flows indicate that the main inter-provincial flows happen between neighboring provinces. Only a few provinces, such as Shanxi, Beijing, Shanghai and Anhui (Reference in Figure Provinces of China), attract migrants move across another provinces. These are also some of the places that attract the most flows and has most inflow population. Places that send the most flows are Shandong and Ningxia. The relationship between inflow population and economic disparities varies in different geographical divisions (reference in Figure Provinces and geographical divisions of China). Positive relationship can be found in south, southwest and northwest China with high-high and low-low values in GDP per capita and gross inflow. However, the relationships change in north, central, east and northeast China. Provinces in low economic status, like Anhui, attract large proportions of population inflow.
